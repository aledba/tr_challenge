Label Taxonomy: Relationships Between Postures

HIERARCHICAL STRUCTURE

Stage (where in process)

On Appeal, Appellate Review, Review of Admin Decision

Motion Type (what action)

Motion to Dismiss, Motion for Summary Judgment, Motion for Attorney's Fees

IS-A

Specific Motion (narrower)

MTD for Lack of SMJ, MTD for Lack of Personal Jurisdiction, MTD for Lack of Standing

DEPENDS-ON

(e.g., Appellate Review (Criminal) requires On Appeal)

Proceeding Type (domain)

Family Law, Criminal, Bankruptcy, Workers' Compensation

KEY FINDINGS FROM CO-OCCURRENCE ANALYSIS

Sparse Labeling (Specific Motions)

IS-A children co-occur with parent only 3-13% of the time

Labelers use the most-specific label only, not both parent and child

e.g., "MTD for Lack of SMJ" appears without "Motion to Dismiss"

Dense Labeling (Stages)

Stage labels co-occur at 89-100% rate

"Appellate Review (Criminal)" nearly always has "On Appeal"

Stage is almost always co-labeled

Why This Matters for Modeling

Confusing IS-A relatives (e.g., predicting "MTD" instead of "MTD for Lack of SMJ") is a less severe error than confusing unrelated postures

Hierarchical loss functions could leverage this structure
